A brief history of video games

From arcade cabinets to cloud streaming, video games have grown from simple experiments into a global entertainment industry.

Early experiments (1950s–1970s)

In the 1950s and 60s, games were small projects on large research computers. Titles like early tennis simulations and text-based adventures showed that computers could be playful, not just mathematical tools.

  • Simple oscilloscopes and mainframes used to display basic graphics
  • Arcade-style games introduced competition and high scores
  • Home consoles began to make games part of everyday life

Home consoles and 3D worlds (1980s–2000s)

Consoles and personal computers made games more accessible. As technology advanced, developers moved from pixel art to 3D graphics and CD-quality sound.

  • Side-scrolling platformers, RPGs, and fighting games became iconic
  • 3D engines made it possible to explore spaces in first-person
  • Games started to include voice acting, cutscenes, and complex stories

Online, indie, and beyond (2000s–today)

Online multiplayer, digital stores, and independent studios have made the game landscape more diverse than ever. Today, players can experience everything from small narrative games to massive open-world adventures.

  • Online games and eSports connect players across the world
  • Indie games explore new art styles and experimental mechanics
  • Streaming and content creation have become part of gaming culture
