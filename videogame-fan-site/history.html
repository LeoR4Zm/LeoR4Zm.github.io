Timeline & milestones

A brief history of video games

From simple experiments on lab computers to global online worlds, video games have grown into one of the most important forms of digital entertainment.

Early arcades & home consoles

In the 1970s and 80s, games lived in arcades and on early home consoles. Simple graphics and sound were enough to create competitive high-score chasing and iconic characters.

  • Cabinets, joysticks, and local multiplayer in arcades
  • Cartridge-based consoles bringing games into the living room
  • Pixel art and chiptune sound as technical limitations and style

The 3D revolution

As hardware improved, games shifted from flat 2D planes into fully 3D worlds. Cameras, analog sticks, and 3D engines changed how players explored spaces.

  • Platformers and action games moved into 3D environments
  • More cinematic cutscenes, voice acting, and complex stories
  • PC games pushed graphics and online connectivity forward

Online, indie, and streaming era

Today, digital stores, online multiplayer, and independent developers have created a huge variety of experiences, from small narrative games to massive open worlds.

  • Always-connected online modes and cooperative play
  • Indie games experimenting with unusual art and storytelling
  • Streaming and content creation shaping how games are discovered
