Character design in games

Memorable characters are more than just good art — they combine visual design, animation, voice acting, and player choice.

Visual silhouettes & readability

Good character design is readable at a glance. The silhouette, color palette, and key shapes tell the player who this character is and what they can do.

  • Distinct shapes help players recognize characters quickly
  • Colors hint at roles: healers, tanks, villains, or mysterious figures
  • Costume details connect to the game’s world and culture

Backstory & player connection

Behind every design, there is a backstory: where the character came from, what they want, and how they change. Even if the player never reads the full lore, good writing shapes the way a character feels on screen.

Playable characters vs. NPCs

Player characters are designed for control and customization, while NPCs often serve to populate the world and push the story forward. Both are essential for immersion.

  • Playable characters often have more animations and equipment options
  • NPCs add life to cities, villages, battlefields, and small side stories
  • Companions can become fan favorites through banter and relationship systems
