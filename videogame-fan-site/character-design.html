Heroes, villains, and NPCs

Character design in games

Great game characters are remembered not only for how they look, but for how they move, speak, and grow as the player spends time with them.

Silhouette & visual identity

A strong silhouette helps players recognize a character instantly, even from far away. Shape language, color, and costume all work together to communicate personality and role.

  • Bold shapes make characters readable in fast-paced scenes
  • Color palettes hint at alignment, class, or faction
  • Details like gear and accessories tell stories without words

Animation & expression

Idle animations, combat moves, and facial expressions bring characters to life. Even small gestures can show nervousness, confidence, or humor.

  • Body language reinforces who a character is
  • Facial animation and camera framing support key story beats
  • Player control must still feel responsive and readable

Backstories & relationships

Lore, dialogue, and player choices shape how we feel about characters. Companions, rivals, and NPCs can become fan favorites based on how they react during the story.

  • Backstories explain motivations and conflicts
  • Relationship systems can create bonds over time
  • Even small NPCs add depth to the world’s culture
