Landmark titles & recognition

Iconic games & game awards

Some series and awards stand out because they changed how we play, how we move through game worlds, or how the industry listens to players.

Assassin’s Creed series

The Assassin’s Creed series is known for its historical settings, parkour-style movement, and open-world stealth gameplay. Each entry drops the player into a different time period, blending fictional stories with real events and cities.

  • Explores eras like the Renaissance, ancient Egypt, and more
  • Combines stealth, combat, and traversal across rooftops and landmarks
  • Uses “present day” framing to connect multiple games into one timeline

Need for Speed series

The Need for Speed series focuses on fast cars, drifting, and street racing energy. It helped define arcade-style racing games with flashy visuals, licensed cars, and high-speed police chases.

  • Emphasis on speed, style, and tuning vehicles
  • Mix of realistic cars with accessible, arcade-style handling
  • Soundtracks and city lighting that make races feel cinematic

Storytelling standouts

Many iconic games are remembered for how they tell stories, not just how they look. Games like Wildermyth show how procedural storytelling and character growth can make each playthrough feel like a personal myth.

  • Characters change over time, gaining scars, traits, and legendary status
  • Events are combined like panels in a comic book, creating a unique tale
  • Player choices and tactical decisions ripple into long-term narrative arcs

Golden Joystick Awards

The Golden Joystick Awards are one of the longest-running game award shows. What makes them especially interesting is that many of the categories are decided by player votes rather than only by a small jury.

  • Highlights which games players themselves are most excited about
  • Includes categories for genres, community support, and overall game of the year
  • Shows how fan opinion and critical reception can both shape a game’s legacy

Awards like this help recognize not only big-budget blockbusters, but also smaller titles that connect strongly with their communities.
