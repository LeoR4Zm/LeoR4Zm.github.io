Landmark series & player recognition

Iconic games & game awards

Some games and series define entire genres, while awards and player votes help show which experiences made the biggest impact in a given year.

Assassin’s Creed series

The Assassin’s Creed series is known for its historical settings, open-world stealth, and parkour-style movement. Each game drops the player into a different time period, blending fictional stories with real cities and events.

  • Explores eras like the Renaissance, ancient Egypt, and more
  • Combines stealth, parkour, and close-quarters combat
  • Uses a modern-day framing device to connect the entire timeline

Need for Speed series

The Need for Speed series focuses on fast cars, drifting, and cinematic street racing. It helped define arcade-style racing with licensed cars, neon-lit cities, and high-speed police chases.

  • Emphasis on speed, style, and vehicle customization
  • Accessible handling, focused on fun rather than full realism
  • Strong use of lighting and music to build “racing at night” atmosphere

Storytelling standouts

Many iconic games are remembered for their characters and narratives. Games like Wildermyth show how procedural storytelling can turn each campaign into a personal myth that feels unique to the player.

  • Characters age, gain traits, and carry scars from past adventures
  • Comic-style story panels adapt to player choices and outcomes
  • Tactical battles and narrative events are tightly linked

Golden Joystick Awards

The Golden Joystick Awards are one of the longest-running game award shows. A key feature is that many categories are decided by player votes, not just by a small panel of critics.

  • Lets players directly support their favorite games of the year
  • Includes categories for genres, community support, and overall GOTY
  • Shows how fan opinion and critical acclaim can both shape a game’s legacy

Why awards matter

Awards and nominations help highlight standout games, but they also reflect what different audiences value. Player-voted awards like the Golden Joysticks may celebrate different titles than jury-only ceremonies.

  • Spotlights smaller or indie games that connect strongly with players
  • Helps developers see which design choices resonated most
  • Becomes part of how future players discover “classics” to try
