People behind the pixels

Developers & studios

Every game is built by teams of designers, programmers, artists, writers, producers, and many more roles working together.

Large studios

Big studios often work on multi-year projects with hundreds of staff members. They build large-scale experiences and long-running series that become household names.

  • Specialized teams for graphics, networking, and engine tech
  • Big marketing campaigns and global releases
  • Pressure to balance creativity with financial risk

Indie developers

Smaller teams and solo developers frequently experiment with unusual mechanics, personal stories, and unique art styles. Many influential games started as indie projects.

  • Shorter games with strong, focused ideas
  • Creative freedom, but small budgets and tight schedules
  • Community support through early access and feedback

Roles on a game team

Game development is highly collaborative. Different disciplines combine technical skills with creativity to build a single experience.

  • Designers plan systems, levels, and player experience
  • Programmers implement gameplay, engine features, and tools
  • Artists, animators, writers, and audio teams shape the world’s look and feel
