Ways to play

Game genres & play styles

Genres help describe what kinds of decisions the player makes — from fast reflexes to slow, thoughtful strategy.

Action & Adventure

These games focus on movement, combat, and exploration. Players often control a character who travels through levels or open worlds, overcoming enemies and obstacles.

Role-Playing Games (RPGs)

RPGs let players grow a character over time by gaining experience, unlocking skills, and making story choices. They often emphasize narrative and worldbuilding.

Strategy & Simulation

Strategy games ask players to plan carefully, manage resources, and think ahead. Simulations try to imitate real-world systems, like cities, farms, or entire civilizations.

Puzzle & Indie Experiments

Puzzle games challenge players with logic, pattern recognition, and creative problem-solving. Many indie titles use puzzle mechanics to explore emotions and abstract ideas.

Multiplayer & eSports

Competitive games, from shooters to MOBAs, are designed around teamwork, quick thinking, and high skill expression. Some become large eSports scenes with professional players.

Cozy & Narrative-Focused

A growing number of games focus on relaxing experiences, relationships, and everyday life. These emphasize atmosphere and story over difficulty.

Example: Sandbox & Creative – Minecraft

Minecraft is a classic example of a sandbox game: instead of following a fixed storyline, players explore, build, and experiment inside a blocky world.

Survival mode asks players to gather resources and stay alive, while creative mode removes danger and lets players focus on construction and redstone machines.
