Play anywhere

Mobile games

Mobile games bring many of the same ideas from console and PC to phones and tablets, but they also have their own design challenges and strengths.

Designed for short sessions

Many mobile games are built around quick sessions that fit into a commute or a break. Interfaces are simplified so that everything works with taps and swipes.

  • Clear UI and large touch targets
  • Levels or runs that only take a few minutes
  • Offline or low-bandwidth play for unstable connections

Variety of genres

Mobile platforms include puzzle games, RPGs, action games, idle games, and more. Some titles are mobile-only, while others are adaptations of existing series.

  • Relaxing puzzle and matching games
  • Mobile RPGs and strategy games with deep systems
  • Multiplayer titles with cross-play on other platforms

Controls & monetization

Designers have to think about virtual buttons, gestures, and how the game should handle free-to-play models, ads, or optional purchases without overwhelming players.

  • Touch controls instead of physical controllers
  • Optional cosmetics and battle passes in some games
  • Accessibility options like auto-play or simplified input
