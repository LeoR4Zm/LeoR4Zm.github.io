Interactive narratives

How games tell stories

Games combine cutscenes, dialogue, player choice, and environmental clues to tell stories that feel personal and reactive.

Linear vs. branching narratives

Some games follow a fixed story path, while others allow multiple endings or routes. Both approaches can be powerful, depending on how they match the game’s goals.

  • Linear stories focus on pacing and cinematic moments
  • Branching stories react to the player’s choices and actions
  • Side quests and optional content add depth to the world

Environmental storytelling

Not all story information is delivered through dialogue. Level design, props, lighting, and background details can quietly suggest history and mood.

An abandoned room, a broken robot, or graffiti on a wall can say just as much as a spoken line of dialogue.
