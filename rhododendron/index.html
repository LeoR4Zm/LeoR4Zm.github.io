Pacific Rhododendron

Rhododendron macrophyllum — Washington’s state flower

Quick Facts

  • Scientific name: Rhododendron macrophyllum
  • Common names: Pacific/Coast rhododendron, California rosebay, bigleaf rhododendron
  • Plant type: Evergreen shrub to small tree
  • Size: typically 3–15 ft; occasionally 20–30 ft in the wild
  • Where it grows: Pacific coast & Cascades—BC to CA, mainly west of the Cascade crest
  • Climate & habitat: Partial shade; moist, acidic, well-drained soils; conifer woods & edges
  • Bloom time (PNW): late spring (often May–June)

About the Plant

The Pacific rhododendron is a broadleaf evergreen native to the Pacific Northwest. In shaded forests it can grow tall and open; at forest edges with more light it stays more compact. Its flowers are usually pink (sometimes white) and appear in showy clusters.

Range & Conditions

Wild populations stretch from British Columbia south to Monterey Bay, CA. You’ll most often find them west of the Cascades in coastal and montane conifer woods. They prefer acidic, well-drained soils, partial shade, and some shelter from wind.
