Mapo Tofu (麻婆豆腐)

A classic Sichuan dish with silky tofu in a spicy, numbing (málà) sauce. Serve hot over steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 400 g medium or firm tofu, cut into 2–3 cm cubes
  • 150 g ground pork (optional; omit for vegetarian)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1–1.5 tbsp doubanjiang (spicy fermented broad bean paste)
  • 1 tsp douchi (fermented black beans), rinsed and minced (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (about 6 g)
  • 10 g ginger, minced
  • 2 scallions, finely sliced (white/green parts separated)
  • 1 tsp ground roasted Sichuan peppercorn (plus more to finish)
  • 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 240 ml low-sodium chicken stock or water
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (slurry)
  • Chili flakes or chili oil to taste (optional)

Directions

  1. Prep tofu (optional but recommended): Bring a small pot of lightly salted water to a bare simmer. Slide in tofu cubes and blanch 1–2 minutes to firm up. Drain gently.
  2. Aromatics: Heat oil in a wok or deep skillet over medium heat. Add ginger, garlic, and scallion whites; stir-fry ~20–30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Paste: Stir in doubanjiang (and douchi if using). Fry 30–45 seconds until the oil turns red.
  4. Protein: Add ground pork and Shaoxing wine. Stir-fry until no longer pink.
  5. Sauce: Add stock, soy sauce, sugar, and a pinch of chili flakes (if using). Bring to a simmer.
  6. Tofu: Gently add tofu. Simmer 3–5 minutes to absorb flavor—avoid vigorous stirring.
  7. Thicken: Stir the cornstarch slurry, drizzle in a little at a time while stirring the sauce gently until glossy and just thickened.
  8. Finish: Sprinkle in ground Sichuan peppercorn and scallion greens. Taste and adjust salt/heat. Serve with rice.

Notes

Control heat with the amount of doubanjiang and chili oil. For vegetarian, omit pork and use vegetable stock; mushrooms add great savor. The signature numbing comes from freshly ground roasted Sichuan peppercorn—add to taste.
