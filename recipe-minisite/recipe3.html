Kung Pao Chicken (宫保鸡丁)

A beloved Sichuan stir-fry: tender chicken, toasty peanuts, dried chilies, and a tangy-sweet sauce with a hint of málà.

Ingredients

Chicken & Aromatics

  • 400 g boneless skinless chicken thighs, diced 1.5–2 cm
  • 8–12 dried red chilies (seeded for less heat)
  • 1 tsp whole Sichuan peppercorn (lightly crushed)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced (about 9 g)
  • 10 g ginger, minced
  • 2 scallions, cut into 2–3 cm pieces
  • 60 g roasted unsalted peanuts (or cashews)
  • 2–3 tbsp neutral oil

Quick Marinade

  • 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp oil

Sauce

  • 1.5 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp dark soy sauce (for color)
  • 1 tbsp Chinkiang black vinegar
  • 1–1.5 tbsp sugar (to taste)
  • 3 tbsp water
  • 1 tsp cornstarch

Directions

  1. Marinate: Toss chicken with soy sauce, wine, sugar, cornstarch, and oil. Let rest 10–15 minutes while you prep aromatics and sauce.
  2. Mix sauce: Whisk all sauce ingredients until smooth; set aside.
  3. Toast spices: Heat oil in a wok over medium. Add dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorn; stir until fragrant (20–30 seconds). Avoid burning.
  4. Stir-fry chicken: Raise to medium-high heat. Add chicken in a single layer; sear, then stir-fry until ~90% cooked.
  5. Aromatics: Add garlic, ginger, and scallions; toss for about 30 seconds.
  6. Sauce & peanuts: Stir the sauce (cornstarch settles), pour it in, and toss until glossy and thickened. Fold in peanuts. Serve immediately.

Notes

Heat level depends on the chilies and peppercorn. For extra texture, add diced bell pepper or celery in step 5. Thigh meat stays juicier than breast—adjust cook time if substituting.
